Colourism in the Caribbean

Hello.

According to Cheryl Grant at VerywillMind, colourism is the practice of favouring lighter skin over darker skin and this preference can be seen within any racial or ethnic background. For people of African decent, it can be as blatant as being denied entry into a restuarant to as subtle as always being the 'background friend' in group pictures.

Dark-skinned girls and boys grow to dislike or even resent the colour of their skin and are often socialised in a way that makes them feel inferior to their lighter counterparts. For example, whether intentional or subconscious, darker-skinned people tend to stand behind their lighter friends. Another example is how many black Caribbean men openly express that they could never love a dark-skinned woman because they do not want their children to be "just as loud and ghetto".

I chose to create this website because I want little boys and girls who look like me to love their skin and grow to see it as their most valued feature, as I have had to learn myself. I want little black boys and girls to understand and believe that they should never be denied any opportunity, undermined or inferioriated or disrespected solely because of their darker colour. I do not intend to generate any profit other than a more afro-appreciative community and region.
